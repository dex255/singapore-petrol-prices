Frequently Asked Questions

Why is diesel currently more expensive than petrol?

Diesel is structurally more vulnerable to supply shocks and heavily relies on crude oil exposed to Middle East disruptions. Supply is also constrained by recent strikes on Russian refineries and export limits from China, all while industrial demand from transport and logistics remains consistently high.

How long will the price gap last?

Analysts anticipate this price difference between diesel and petrol to persist for months, and potentially stretch over a year. The underlying supply issues are structural, meaning restoring global flows will take significant time.

How does this affect consumer prices?

Rising diesel costs increase freight, logistics, and production expenses. This inevitably leads to more expensive goods and food. Although the full impact may take months to manifest, it will definitely add inflationary pressure on households.

Is the world facing a diesel shortage?

While there is no outright shortage currently, global supplies are running tight. Low inventories combined with geopolitical instability mean that securing additional diesel supplies has become significantly more difficult and costly.
